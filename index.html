📋 Use one of the four ways to survey customer preferences

Timing suggestion: Select the right moment in the customer journey

Customer Satisfaction (CSAT)

Core Question: "How satisfied were you with your recent experience/purchase?"

Scale Used: 1 to 5 stars, or a 1–5 scale ranging from "Very Dissatisfied" to "Very Satisfied."

Best Timing: Triggered immediately after a specific interaction, like a customer support ticket resolution or a product delivery.

Net Promoter Score (NPS)

Core Question: "How likely are you to recommend our company/product to a friend or colleague?"

Scale Used: 0 to 10 points.

Scoring Logic: Respondents are categorized into Detractors (0–6), Passives (7–8), and Promoters (9–10).

Best Timing: Sent quarterly or bi-annually to measure overall brand loyalty rather than a single interaction.

Customer Effort Score (CES)

Core Question: "To what extent do you agree with the statement: The company made it easy for me to handle my issue?"

Scale Used: 1 to 7 scale, from "Strongly Disagree" to "Strongly Agree."

Best Timing: Sent immediately after self-service actions, website checkouts, or onboarding steps to find friction points.

Product-Market Fit (PMF) / Sean Ellis Survey

Core Question: "How would you feel if you could no longer use this product?"

Scale Used: Three choices: "Very Disappointed", "Somewhat Disappointed", or "Not Disappointed."

Best Timing: Handed out to early adopters who have used the product at least a few times to gauge demand.
